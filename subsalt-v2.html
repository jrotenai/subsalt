The Problem

Health data access is
broken by design.

IRB queues, manual de-identification, and legal review aren't bugs. They're baked into how healthcare data governance was built.

01

Data access is the bottleneck

IRB approvals, IT security reviews, and manual de-identification pipelines slow teams down before they write a single query. Weeks or months of waiting before any exploration begins.

02

Compliance exposure grows with access

The more teams that touch sensitive data, the larger the attack surface. Most de-identification today is an afterthought: a pipeline bolted on after the fact, fragile and hard to audit.

03

Privacy infrastructure is expensive to build

In-house anonymization pipelines are brittle and rarely meet the bar that legal needs to sign off on. Building it yourself doesn't scale, and it doesn't get safer over time.

How It Works

De-identification at
the query layer.

Subsalt sits between your data scientists and sensitive data. The query goes in. Legally de-identified synthetic results come out. Real data never moves.

01

Write standard SQL

Your team writes the same Postgres-compatible queries they always have. No new syntax, no new tooling. Subsalt is transparent to every existing workflow: Jupyter, dbt, Tableau, anything Postgres-compatible.

02

Subsalt intercepts and de-identifies

The query engine processes your request against real data inside the secure boundary. It generates statistically valid synthetic results before anything is returned. PHI never leaves the boundary.

03

Receive compliant output

Your team gets results they can use for model training, analysis, and product development, without touching PHI. Every query is logged with a full audit trail your compliance team can rely on.
